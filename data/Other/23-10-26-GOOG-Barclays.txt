Google (GOOGL) Receives Mixed Q3 Report: Search Revenue Accelerates, Cloud Disappoints
Barclays's Ross Sandler lowered their price target on Google (NASDAQ: GOOGL) by 10% from $200 to $180 on 2023/10/25. The analyst maintained their Strong Buy rating on the stock.

In analyzing Google's Q3 report, Sandler described it as a "classic mixed bag." The company showcased impressive revenue figures from Search and YouTube, surpassing market expectations. However, there were some setbacks, including slightly missed margins and a disappointing performance in the Cloud segment, which Sandler referred to as a "bad whiff."

Despite the disappointment in Cloud, Sandler emphasized that the "nice acceleration" in Search revenue was the most significant takeaway from the Q3 report.

Other analysts also chimed in with their updates on Google (GOOGL) on October 25, 2023. Brian Nowak from Morgan Stanley lowered their price target by 3.2% to $155 but maintained their Strong Buy rating on the stock. Piper Sandler's Thomas Champion, on the other hand, raised their price target by 2% to $147 and also maintained their Strong Buy rating. Needham's Laura Martin increased their price target by 14.3% to $140 and maintained a Buy rating.

According to data from WallStreetZen, 89.5% of top-rated analysts currently rate GOOGL as a Strong Buy or Buy. Only 10.5% see it as a Hold, and no analysts recommend or strongly recommend selling the stock.

Looking ahead, analysts have a consensus forecast that Google (GOOGL) will deliver earnings per share (EPS) of $5.62 in the upcoming year. If these predictions hold true, it would represent an 18% increase in yearly EPS on a year-over-year basis.

In terms of stock performance, Google (GOOGL) has seen substantial growth. Year-over-year, the stock has surged by 35.4%, outpacing the S&P 500, which has risen by 10.3% during the same period.

Barclays analyst Ross Sandler holds an impressive ranking among Wall Street analysts. With an average return of 1.1% and a win rate of 48%, Sandler is ranked in the top 20% out of 4,369 analysts. They specialize in analyzing the Technology and Consumer Cyclical sectors, among others.

Google, operated by Alphabet Inc., is a multinational technology company that offers a wide range of products and platforms. The company operates through three segments: Google Services, Google Cloud, and Other Bets. Google Services includes popular products and services such as ads, Android, Chrome, Gmail, Google Drive, Google Maps, Google Photos, Google Play, Search, and YouTube. Google Cloud provides infrastructure, platform, and other services, while the Other Bets segment focuses on selling health technology and internet services. Founded in 1998 and headquartered in Mountain View, California, Google operates globally, serving markets in the United States, Europe, the Middle East, Africa, the Asia-Pacific, Canada, and Latin America.
